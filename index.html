The History of Vending Machines

The invention of the vending machine started many decades ago. In 17th century England, a primitive version of a vending machine was developed to help those that were working in iron furnaces during their lunch break. A simple way to get food was needed as workers couldn't leave for lunch at noon and return shortly after.

A piece of bread with a slot cut into it would be dispensed from a container when money was paid -- making it one of the first "coin operated" devices ever created.

In 1868, an American invented another type of vending machine – this time selling gum on New York train platforms. As time went by, more and more companies were inventing ways to sell their products through automated machines instead of employees. Thefirst machines were large and expensive, but as time went on they became smaller and more affordable.

In 2017, vending machines have been completely transformed from those that were first invented centuries ago.
